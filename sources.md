## C. Regulatory / ANVISA  ⚠️ verify all specifics with a Brazil-side regulatory partner

- **[S12] [Confirm]** ANVISA classifies cosmetics by **risk grade**: **Grade 1** (lower risk → *notification* to GGCOS) vs **Grade 2** (higher risk → *registration* with full dossier incl. stability/safety/efficacy). Global Regulatory Partners — https://globalregulatorypartners.com/cosmetic-or-grade-2-understand-the-categories-and-avoid-rework/
- **[S13] [Confirm]** Relevant resolutions cited by consultancies: **RDC 752/2022** (product categorization, partly superseded), **RDC 907/2024** (labeling), **RDC 949/2024** (risk classification). Exact current numbers must be confirmed against ANVISA/gov.br. Global Regulatory Partners — https://globalregulatorypartners.com/registering-cosmetics-with-anvisa-practice-guide-for-2025/ ; GPC Gateway — https://gpcgateway.com/regulatory-regions/brazil/news-detail/brazil-revises-cosmetics-regulation-with-the-introduction-of-rdc-9072024-MTU1Nw==
- **[S14] [Confirm]** Foreign companies **cannot register directly**; must appoint a **Brazilian importer / legal representative** holding **AFE (Autorização de Funcionamento de Empresa)**, an **Operating License** (local Vigilância Sanitária), and **GMP** evidence. Nova Trade Brasil — https://novatradebrasil.com/en/anvisa-regulatory-requirements/
- **[S15] [Confirm]** **Portuguese labeling** required: product name, brand, AFE number, lot, expiry, country of origin, importer/manufacturer details, ingredients (INCI), category warnings (per RDC 907/2024). Kasey Beauty — https://www.kaseybeauty.com/what-are-the-cosmetics-labelling-requirements-in-brazil/ ; CGM Law — https://www.cgmlaw.com.br/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/www.cgmlaw.com.br-rascunho-automatico-navigating-anvisa-regulations.pdf
- **[S16] [Confirm]** Cosmetics import clearance runs through **PAFCO** (ANVISA sanitary post) via **Portal Único Siscomex** (Electronic Import Petition). Nova Trade Brasil — https://novatradebrasil.com/en/anvisa-regulatory-requirements/

## D. Logistics, Customs & Taxes  ⚠️ exact rates depend on NCM + state + tax reform

- **[S17] [Confirm]** Cosmetics fall under **NCM Chapter 33**. Indicative Brazilian import taxes: **II 0–35%**, **IPI 0–30%**, **ICMS 17–25% (state)**, **PIS/COFINS ~11.75% general** but **higher for cosmetics/perfumes** (can reach ~15–20%+). Taxes cascade on CIF. U.S. ITA / trade.gov — https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/brazil-import-tariffs ; PwC Tax Summaries — https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/brazil/corporate/other-taxes
- **[S18] [Confirm]** **Tax reform under way**: EC 132/2023 + LC 214/2025 introduce **CBS** (replacing PIS/COFINS/most IPI) and **IBS** (replacing ICMS) over a ~7-year transition from 2026. Nova Trade Brasil — https://novatradebrasil.com/en/import-duties-taxes-brazil/
- **[S19] [Confirmed]** Import requires **SISCOMEX / Portal Único** registration; **DUIMP** is replacing legacy import declarations. U.S. ITA / trade.gov — https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/brazil-import-requirements-and-documentation ; Santander Trade — https://santandertrade.com/en/portal/analyse-markets/brazil/customs-procedures
- **[S20] [Confirmed]** Core customs documents: **Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Bill of Lading / AWB, Certificate of Origin**, plus ANVISA approval/notification docs and product labels/ingredient lists. U.S. ITA / trade.gov — https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/brazil-import-requirements-and-documentation
